Help with answering this

Member

If you are:

  • currently paying into the Scheme, you will be an active member
  • already getting your pension payments, you will be a pensioner member
  • no longer paying into the scheme and not getting your pension payments, you will be a deferred member

About NHS Pension membership types

Employer

If you select employer, you should be employed by an NHS organisation or a:

  • direction body
  • approved non-NHS organisation
  • smaller NHS organisation, such as a GP practice

Acting for a member

To speak to us on behalf of a member you must have their permission.

They can grant this by nominating you in:

  • a Letter of Authority to be able to contact us to discuss and receive information about their pension
  • a legal power of attorney to act on their behalf if they can no longer manage their own affairs

About power of attorney and Letter of Authority

Telling us someone has died

You only need to contact NHS Pensions if the person who has died was not actively employed in the NHS

We'll ask you for their:

  • National Insurance number
  • NHS Pensions membership number

You do not need to contact us if:

  • the person who has died was actively employed in the NHS – contact their employer instead
  • you have already used Tell Us Once to report their death
