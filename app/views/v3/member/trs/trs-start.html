There are 2 types of statement you may receive as a member of the NHS Pension Scheme.

These are the:

  • Annual Benefit Statement (ABS)
  • Total Reward Statement (TRS)

which you receive depends on your circumstances.

Annual Benefit Statement (ABS)

You will receive an Annual Benefit Statement on its own through My NHS Pension. Or as part of a Total Reward Statement through the Electronic Staff Record (ESR).

Your ABS will show:

  • What your pension is worth (its current value)
  • What you could get (estimated retirement income)

You will receive only an ABS if you are either:

  • no longer working for the NHS and not paying in to the NHS Pension Scheme
  • working for direction bodies, approved non-NHS organisations, or smaller NHS organisations
  • working for NHS organisations which do not use ESR

Total Reward Statement (TRS)

If you are currently working in the NHS and can access ESR you will receive a Total Reward Statement. A TRS is an overall rewards package.

It includes an Annual Benefit Statement for members of the NHS Pension Scheme as well as information on your employer benefits.

You will receive a TRS if you are:

  • currently working for the NHS
  • currently paying in to the NHS Pension Scheme
  • able to access ESR
